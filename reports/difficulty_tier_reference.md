# Course Difficulty Tier Reference Guide

This guide helps determine the appropriate difficulty tier language for course analysis reports.

## Easy Courses (35 total)

### Difficulty Tiers by Ranking:

**BRUTAL-EASY** (Ranks 1-5)
- Rank 1-3: "brutal easy course" 
- Rank 4-5: "very challenging easy course"
- Language: "among the most difficult easy courses"
- Examples: LBE (Labyrinth), EDE (El Dorado), TSE (Tethys Station)

**CHALLENGING-EASY** (Ranks 6-12)
- Rank 6-9: "challenging easy course"
- Rank 10-12: "moderate-challenging easy course" 
- Language: "more challenging than typical easy courses"
- Examples: AME, RCE, ILE, GBE, VNE, BBE, CBE

**MODERATE-EASY** (Ranks 13-23)
- Rank 13-18: "moderate-easy course"
- Rank 19-23: "typical easy course"
- Language: "middle-tier difficulty among easy courses"
- Examples: QVE, SWE, MOE, SLE, MYE, HHE, ELE, ALE, 20E, UTE, 8BE, WWE

**EASY** (Ranks 24-35)
- Rank 24-29: "easy course"
- Rank 30-35: "very easy course"
- Language: "among the easier courses in the easy category"
- Examples: MGE, AWE, MWE, TTE, ATE, CLE, JCE, OGE, LLE, TOE, WGE

## Hard Courses (35 total)

### Difficulty Tiers by Ranking:

**NIGHTMARE** (Ranks 1-5)
- Rank 1-3: "nightmare hard course"
- Rank 4-5: "brutal hard course"
- Language: "among the most punishing courses in the game"
- Examples: SSH (Seagull Stacks), CBH (Cherry Blossom Hard), BBH (Bogey's Bonanza Hard)

**VERY-HARD** (Ranks 6-12)
- Rank 6-9: "very hard course"
- Rank 10-12: "challenging hard course"
- Language: "significantly more difficult than typical hard courses"
- Examples: TSH, SLH, GBH, LBH, OGH, RCH, EDH, AMH, TTH

**MODERATE-HARD** (Ranks 13-23)
- Rank 13-18: "moderate-hard course"
- Rank 19-23: "typical hard course"
- Language: "middle-tier difficulty among hard courses"
- Examples: ZZH, ATH, MOH, UTH, 8BH, QVH, MYH, SWH, 20H, WWH, JCH

**MANAGEABLE-HARD** (Ranks 24-35)
- Rank 24-29: "manageable hard course"
- Rank 30-35: "easier hard course"
- Language: "among the more approachable hard courses"
- Examples: MGH, VNH, AWH, ILH, HHH, ELH, TOH, MWH, ALH, WGH, CLH, LLH

## Comparison Language Templates

### Easy Course Comparisons:
- **Brutal-Easy**: "more challenging than courses like [easier examples] but not as punishing as [harder examples]"
- **Moderate-Easy**: "sits in the middle tier of easy course difficulty - more challenging than truly easy courses like [examples] but much more manageable than brutal easy courses like [examples]"
- **Easy**: "among the easier courses - much more forgiving than challenging courses like [examples]"

### Hard Course Comparisons:
- **Nightmare**: "among the most punishing courses in the game - makes even difficult courses like [examples] look manageable"
- **Moderate-Hard**: "sits in the middle tier of hard course difficulty - challenging but not the nightmare that courses like [examples] represent"
- **Manageable-Hard**: "among the more approachable hard courses - much more forgiving than brutal courses like [examples]"

## Strategic Language by Tier

### Easy Courses:
- **Brutal-Easy**: "demands respect despite being an easy course", "can punish mistakes severely"
- **Moderate-Easy**: "rewards precision and patience", "offers scoring opportunities with careful play"
- **Easy**: "great scoring opportunity", "should be a confidence builder"

### Hard Courses:
- **Nightmare**: "approach with maximum respect", "survival mode", "damage control is key"
- **Moderate-Hard**: "demands careful play", "still challenging but manageable with good strategy"
- **Manageable-Hard**: "approachable with solid fundamentals", "good opportunity to score well on a hard course"

## Usage Notes:
- Always compare within the same difficulty category (Easy vs Easy, Hard vs Hard)
- Reference specific course codes when possible for context
- Use emoji appropriately: 🔥 for danger, 🌸 for moderate, 🍀 for easier
- Adjust language based on the specific course's reputation and history
